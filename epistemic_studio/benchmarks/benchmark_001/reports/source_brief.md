# Source Brief

- Universal Intelligence: A Definition of Machine Intelligence (https://arxiv.org/abs/0712.3329): AGI as broad performance across environments.
- World Models (https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.10122): World model as learned compressed predictive environment representation.
- A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence (https://openreview.net/pdf?id=BZ5a1r-kVsf): Position that autonomous intelligence needs predictive world models.
- The Bitter Lesson (https://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html): Counter-pressure against hand-built structure; scalable search and learning dominate.
- Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165): Scaling language models yields broad task adaptation without explicit task modules.
- Sparks of Artificial General Intelligence (https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.12712): Evidence that broad capabilities can emerge from large language models, with caveats.
