# Equivalence Map

## c01_repeated_interaction
Cumulative knowledge requires repeated interaction between system and environment.

- Novelty class: already_known
- Closest equivalents:
  - reinforcement_learning: [Sutton and Barto, Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf)
  - cybernetics: [Wiener, Cybernetics: Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine](https://direct.mit.edu/books/oa-monograph/4581/Cybernetics-or-Control-and-Communication-in-the)
  - control_theory: [Kalman filtering overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalman_filter)
  - statistical_learning_theory: [Valiant PAC learning overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probably_approximately_correct_learning)
- Partial overlaps:
  - bayesian_epistemology: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  - active_inference: [Free energy principle and active inference overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_energy_principle)
- Genuine difference: The current theory states the condition at a higher abstraction level across biological, institutional, computational, and organizational systems.
- Assessment: Repeated sampling, feedback, recursive estimation, and interaction are core assumptions in existing learning, control, and cybernetic frameworks.

## c02_retention
Cumulative knowledge requires retention: prior interaction must affect later behavior.

- Novelty class: improved_formulation
- Closest equivalents:
  - control_theory: [Kalman filtering overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalman_filter)
  - bayesian_epistemology: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  - reinforcement_learning: [Sutton and Barto, Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf)
  - knowledge_management: [Nonaka and Takeuchi SECI model overview](https://ascnhighered.org/ASCN/change_theories/collection/seci.html)
- Partial overlaps:
  - distributed_systems: [CAP theorem overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem)
  - information_theory: [Shannon, A Mathematical Theory of Communication](https://people.math.harvard.edu/~ctm/home/text/others/shannon/entropy/entropy.pdf)
- Genuine difference: The theory treats memory as substrate-neutral: parameters, records, genes, prices, practices, and institutions can all instantiate retention.
- Assessment: The core idea is old, but the substrate-neutral organizational framing usefully prevents confusing human-readable archives with memory itself.

## c03_truth_correlated_feedback
Feedback must be truth-correlated rather than merely present.

- Novelty class: stronger_formulation
- Closest equivalents:
  - cybernetics: [Wiener, Cybernetics: Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine](https://direct.mit.edu/books/oa-monograph/4581/Cybernetics-or-Control-and-Communication-in-the)
  - reinforcement_learning: [Sutton and Barto, Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf)
  - bayesian_epistemology: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  - control_theory: [Kalman filtering overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalman_filter)
- Partial overlaps:
  - philosophy_of_science: [Popper, Conjectures and Refutations](https://philpapers.org/rec/POPCAR-5)
  - active_inference: [Free energy principle and active inference overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_energy_principle)
- Genuine difference: The theory packages feedback alignment as a primitive axiom for any cumulative knowledge system, not only as a loss/reward design issue.
- Assessment: Misleading signals and bad rewards are well known; making truth-correlation a cross-domain axiom is a useful unification, not a clear discovery.

## c04_scope_control
Reliable knowledge requires scope control: retained claims must carry conditions of transfer.

- Novelty class: stronger_formulation
- Closest equivalents:
  - statistical_learning_theory: [Valiant PAC learning overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Probably_approximately_correct_learning)
  - statistical_learning_theory: [Blumer et al., Learnability and the Vapnik-Chervonenkis Dimension](https://www.cis.upenn.edu/~danroth/Teaching/CS446-17/Papers/p929-blumer.pdf)
  - philosophy_of_science: [Kuhn, The Structure of Scientific Revolutions](https://www.lri.fr/~mbl/Stanford/CS477/papers/Kuhn-SSR-2ndEd.pdf)
- Partial overlaps:
  - reinforcement_learning: [Sutton and Barto, Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf)
  - bayesian_epistemology: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  - distributed_systems: [CAP theorem overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem)
- Genuine difference: The theory makes applicability boundaries a first-class requirement rather than a downstream caveat.
- Assessment: Domain restrictions, hypothesis classes, external validity, and paradigm-relative puzzles are established; the novelty is the operational insistence that scope must be retained with knowledge artifacts.

Nonzero cost makes compression, reuse, and prioritization epistemically relevant.

- Novelty class: improved_formulation
- Closest equivalents:

  - information_theory: [Grunwald, A Tutorial Introduction to the Minimum Description Length Principle](https://homepages.cwi.nl/~paulv/course-kc/mdlintro.pdf)

  - distributed_systems: [CAP theorem overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem)

  - knowledge_management: [Nonaka and Takeuchi SECI model overview](https://ascnhighered.org/ASCN/change_theories/collection/seci.html)
- Genuine difference: The theory links cost pressure to organizational attention and research strategy, not just coding length or sample complexity.

## c06_update_stability
Cumulative knowledge requires update stability: learning must not destroy, invert, or endlessly oscillate around valid retained knowledge faster than correction can recover it.

- Novelty class: potentially_novel
- Closest equivalents:
  - control_theory: [Kalman filtering overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalman_filter)
  - reinforcement_learning: [Sutton and Barto, Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf)
  - distributed_systems: [CAP theorem overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem)
  - organizational_learning: [Argyris and Schon single-loop and double-loop learning overview](https://www.open.edu/openlearn/mod/oucontent/view.php?id=135424&section=4.2)
  - evolutionary_theory: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Natural Selection](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/natural-selection/)
- Partial overlaps:
  - bayesian_epistemology: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  - knowledge_management: [Nonaka and Takeuchi SECI model overview](https://ascnhighered.org/ASCN/change_theories/collection/seci.html)
- Genuine difference: The theory elevates stability of updates from an engineering property to a minimal axiom for cumulative knowledge.
- Assessment: Every ingredient has prior art, but the exact necessity claim across all epistemic substrates appears less directly covered.

## c07_representation_theorem
Any cumulative knowledge system can be represented as retained state, environment interaction, update operator, truth-correlated evaluation, scope relation, cost pressure, and stability constraint.

- Novelty class: potentially_novel
- Closest equivalents:
  - control_theory: [Kalman filtering overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalman_filter)
  - reinforcement_learning: [Sutton and Barto, Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf)
  - bayesian_epistemology: [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, Bayesian Epistemology](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-bayesian/)
  - cybernetics: [Wiener, Cybernetics: Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine](https://direct.mit.edu/books/oa-monograph/4581/Cybernetics-or-Control-and-Communication-in-the)
- Partial overlaps:
  - active_inference: [Free energy principle and active inference overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_energy_principle)
  - knowledge_management: [Nonaka and Takeuchi SECI model overview](https://ascnhighered.org/ASCN/change_theories/collection/seci.html)
  - distributed_systems: [CAP theorem overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAP_theorem)
- Genuine difference: The schema is intentionally minimal and cross-substrate; it avoids assuming probabilities, symbols, rewards, agents, or explicit theories.
- Assessment: This is mostly a synthesis. It becomes novel only if it predicts failures outside the home domains of existing frameworks better than those frameworks do.
