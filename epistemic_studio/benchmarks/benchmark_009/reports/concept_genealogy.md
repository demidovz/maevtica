# Concept Genealogy

## Entropy
- Domain: thermodynamics
- Approximate birth: 1850s
- Pre-concept world: Heat was analyzed through engines, temperature, caloric remnants, and reversible cycles. Carnot had a performance limit, but no state quantity explained irreversible direction.
- Existing representation failed because: Heat-engine limits, irreversibility, and temperature scales could not be compressed by a simple substance-like heat representation.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A conserved-or-monotonic state quantity for heat transformations that tracks unavailable work and distinguishes reversible from irreversible processes.
- Historical solution: Entropy as a state function whose change organizes reversible heat transfer and the directionality of thermodynamic processes.
- Structural similarity: 0.86
- Concept value score: 0.831
- Sources:
  - [A History of Thermodynamics: The Missing Manual](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7516509/)
  - [Second law of thermodynamics overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_law_of_thermodynamics)

## Gene
- Domain: biology
- Approximate birth: 1909
- Pre-concept world: Heredity was described through traits, breeding ratios, germ plasm, and Mendelian factors, but visible traits and inherited determinants were conflated.
- Existing representation failed because: Breeding regularities required a stable transmissible unit distinct from appearance, development, and environment.
- Generated candidate before reveal: An abstract hereditary unit separated from observable trait expression, allowing inheritance to be tracked independently from phenotype.
- Historical solution: Gene as a unit of heredity, paired with genotype/phenotype distinctions.
- Structural similarity: 0.91
- Concept value score: 0.796
- Sources:
  - [1909: The Word Gene Coined](https://www.genome.gov/25520244/online-education-kit-1909-the-word-gene-coined)
  - [The holist tradition in twentieth century genetics](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4048101/)

## Field
- Domain: physics
- Approximate birth: 1830s-1860s
- Pre-concept world: Electricity and magnetism were often represented through sources, forces, action at a distance, fluids, and mechanical analogies.
- Existing representation failed because: Induction, propagation, and spatial patterns between sources made source-only representation awkward.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A spatially distributed physical state assigning influence to every point, so the region between bodies becomes part of the explanation.
- Historical solution: Field as a real or mathematically represented spatial quantity mediating electromagnetic phenomena.
- Structural similarity: 0.89
- Concept value score: 0.838
- Sources:
  - [The field concepts of Faraday and Maxwell](https://www.ifi.unicamp.br/~assis/The-field-concepts-of-Faraday-and-Maxwell%282009%29.pdf)
  - [History of classical field theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_classical_field_theory)

## Information
- Domain: communication engineering
- Approximate birth: 1948
- Pre-concept world: Telegraph and telephone engineering had bandwidth, signals, noise, and codes, but lacked a general measure independent of message meaning.
- Existing representation failed because: Communication systems needed a meaning-independent quantity governing compression, redundancy, noise, and capacity.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A statistical measure of uncertainty reduction in a message source, tied to channel limits and coding efficiency.
- Historical solution: Information entropy, channel capacity, redundancy, and coding theorems.
- Structural similarity: 0.93
- Concept value score: 0.870
- Sources:
  - [Shannon, A Mathematical Theory of Communication](https://people.math.harvard.edu/~ctm/home/text/others/shannon/entropy/entropy.pdf)
  - [Information theory overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_theory)

## Natural Selection
- Domain: evolutionary biology
- Approximate birth: 1859
- Pre-concept world: Species resemblance, adaptation, domestication, fossils, and biogeography were known, but species fixity and separate creation left patterns disconnected.
- Existing representation failed because: Adapted complexity and branching diversity required a mechanism linking small heritable variation to large-scale change.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A population-level filter that accumulates heritable variations because some variants reproduce more successfully in local conditions.
- Historical solution: Natural selection as differential survival and reproduction of heritable variation.
- Structural similarity: 0.90
- Concept value score: 0.853
- Sources:
  - [Darwin Online: On the Origin of Species](https://darwin-online.org.uk/contents.html#origin)
  - [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: Natural Selection](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/natural-selection/)

## Space-Time
- Domain: relativity
- Approximate birth: 1907-1908
- Pre-concept world: Electrodynamics and mechanics had separate space and time variables. Lorentz transformations worked mathematically, but their geometry was not yet the primitive representation.
- Existing representation failed because: Relativity made separate absolute space and absolute time a poor coordinate system for invariant physical laws.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A unified geometric manifold where space and time coordinates transform together and invariant intervals replace separate absolutes.
- Historical solution: Minkowski spacetime as a four-dimensional geometric structure for relativity.
- Structural similarity: 0.88
- Concept value score: 0.830
- Sources:
  - [The Historical Origins of Spacetime](https://shs.hal.science/halshs-01234449/document)
  - [Hermann Minkowski's Spacetime](https://galileo-unbound.blog/2021/04/24/hermann-minkowskis-spacetime-the-theory-that-einstein-overlooked/)

## Algorithm / Computability
- Domain: mathematics and computer science
- Approximate birth: 1930s formalization
- Pre-concept world: Mathematicians used procedures, calculability, proof search, and decision problems, but effective procedure was not sharply delimited.
- Existing representation failed because: Hilbert-style decision questions required a representation of what any mechanical method could do.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A formal machine-like procedure with discrete states and steps that defines effective calculability and its limits.
- Historical solution: Turing-machine computability and related formalizations of algorithmic procedure.
- Structural similarity: 0.87
- Concept value score: 0.854
- Sources:
  - [Algorithms: From Al-Khwarizmi to Turing and Beyond](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/303329677_Algorithms_From_Al-Khwarizmi_to_Turing_and_Beyond)
  - [Turing, On Computable Numbers](https://www.cs.virginia.edu/~robins/Turing_Paper_1936.pdf)

## Probability
- Domain: mathematics
- Approximate birth: 1650s
- Pre-concept world: Uncertainty existed in gambling, law, insurance, and astronomy, but there was no general calculus of chance or fair expectation.
- Existing representation failed because: Fair division and risk pricing required a quantitative representation of uncertain possibilities.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A normalized measure over possible outcomes that supports fair expectation and systematic calculation under uncertainty.
- Historical solution: Probability and expectation as mathematical tools for chance and uncertain judgment.
- Structural similarity: 0.88
- Concept value score: 0.819
- Sources:
  - [History of probability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_probability)
  - [Pascal and the Invention of Probability Theory](https://www.ms.uky.edu/~dmu228/ma320/pascal_invention_probability.pdf)

## Energy
- Domain: physics
- Approximate birth: 1840s-1850s
- Pre-concept world: Mechanics had work, vis viva, heat, chemical affinity, electricity, and motion, but no accepted conserved transformable quantity spanning them.
- Existing representation failed because: Conversions among heat, work, electricity, and motion demanded a common accounting variable.
- Generated candidate before reveal: A conserved transformable quantity that appears in multiple forms and permits cross-domain accounting.
- Historical solution: Energy as a conserved quantity unifying mechanical work, heat, and other physical processes.
- Structural similarity: 0.92
- Concept value score: 0.861
- Sources:
  - [Conservation of Energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy)
  - [Conservation of Energy: Missing Features in Its Nature and Justification](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8570307/)
