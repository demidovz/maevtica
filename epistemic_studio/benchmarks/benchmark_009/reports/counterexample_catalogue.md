# Counterexample Catalogue

## Discovery of Neptune
- Type: major discovery without new primitive
- Why it matters: It improved astronomy through prediction from existing Newtonian mechanics rather than by inventing a new representation.
- Result: Concept genesis is not required for every major discovery; some discoveries exploit existing concepts with high precision.

## Higgs boson detection
- Type: fact confirmation rather than concept birth
- Why it matters: The experimental discovery confirmed a theoretical entity already embedded in an existing conceptual framework.
- Result: Large epistemic gain can occur without a new primitive at discovery time.

## Phlogiston
- Type: low-value invented concept
- Why it matters: It compressed some combustion language temporarily but blocked better oxygen and conservation-based representations.
- Result: A concept can be useful locally yet lose long-term value if it fails cross-domain transfer and predictive correction.

## Vital force
- Type: low-value invented concept
- Why it matters: It named biological difficulty without producing enough durable prediction or mechanistic compression.
- Result: Naming a gap is not concept birth unless the representation increases compression and future discovery.

Counterexample result: concept genesis is not identical to discovery, confirmation, naming, or explanation. The theory survives only if it predicts when new primitives are needed rather than treating every scientific advance as concept birth.
