# Historical Reconstruction Report

## Entropy
Before the concept, researchers could say: Heat was analyzed through engines, temperature, caloric remnants, and reversible cycles. Carnot had a performance limit, but no state quantity explained irreversible direction.
They could not yet cleanly say: Entropy as a state function whose change organizes reversible heat transfer and the directionality of thermodynamic processes.
The pressure was: Heat-engine limits, irreversibility, and temperature scales could not be compressed by a simple substance-like heat representation.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A conserved-or-monotonic state quantity for heat transformations that tracks unavailable work and distinguishes reversible from irreversible processes.

## Gene
Before the concept, researchers could say: Heredity was described through traits, breeding ratios, germ plasm, and Mendelian factors, but visible traits and inherited determinants were conflated.
They could not yet cleanly say: Gene as a unit of heredity, paired with genotype/phenotype distinctions.
The pressure was: Breeding regularities required a stable transmissible unit distinct from appearance, development, and environment.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: An abstract hereditary unit separated from observable trait expression, allowing inheritance to be tracked independently from phenotype.

## Field
Before the concept, researchers could say: Electricity and magnetism were often represented through sources, forces, action at a distance, fluids, and mechanical analogies.
They could not yet cleanly say: Field as a real or mathematically represented spatial quantity mediating electromagnetic phenomena.
The pressure was: Induction, propagation, and spatial patterns between sources made source-only representation awkward.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A spatially distributed physical state assigning influence to every point, so the region between bodies becomes part of the explanation.

## Information
Before the concept, researchers could say: Telegraph and telephone engineering had bandwidth, signals, noise, and codes, but lacked a general measure independent of message meaning.
They could not yet cleanly say: Information entropy, channel capacity, redundancy, and coding theorems.
The pressure was: Communication systems needed a meaning-independent quantity governing compression, redundancy, noise, and capacity.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A statistical measure of uncertainty reduction in a message source, tied to channel limits and coding efficiency.

## Natural Selection
Before the concept, researchers could say: Species resemblance, adaptation, domestication, fossils, and biogeography were known, but species fixity and separate creation left patterns disconnected.
They could not yet cleanly say: Natural selection as differential survival and reproduction of heritable variation.
The pressure was: Adapted complexity and branching diversity required a mechanism linking small heritable variation to large-scale change.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A population-level filter that accumulates heritable variations because some variants reproduce more successfully in local conditions.

## Space-Time
Before the concept, researchers could say: Electrodynamics and mechanics had separate space and time variables. Lorentz transformations worked mathematically, but their geometry was not yet the primitive representation.
They could not yet cleanly say: Minkowski spacetime as a four-dimensional geometric structure for relativity.
The pressure was: Relativity made separate absolute space and absolute time a poor coordinate system for invariant physical laws.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A unified geometric manifold where space and time coordinates transform together and invariant intervals replace separate absolutes.

## Algorithm / Computability
Before the concept, researchers could say: Mathematicians used procedures, calculability, proof search, and decision problems, but effective procedure was not sharply delimited.
They could not yet cleanly say: Turing-machine computability and related formalizations of algorithmic procedure.
The pressure was: Hilbert-style decision questions required a representation of what any mechanical method could do.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A formal machine-like procedure with discrete states and steps that defines effective calculability and its limits.

## Probability
Before the concept, researchers could say: Uncertainty existed in gambling, law, insurance, and astronomy, but there was no general calculus of chance or fair expectation.
They could not yet cleanly say: Probability and expectation as mathematical tools for chance and uncertain judgment.
The pressure was: Fair division and risk pricing required a quantitative representation of uncertain possibilities.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A normalized measure over possible outcomes that supports fair expectation and systematic calculation under uncertainty.

## Energy
Before the concept, researchers could say: Mechanics had work, vis viva, heat, chemical affinity, electricity, and motion, but no accepted conserved transformable quantity spanning them.
They could not yet cleanly say: Energy as a conserved quantity unifying mechanical work, heat, and other physical processes.
The pressure was: Conversions among heat, work, electricity, and motion demanded a common accounting variable.
The blind candidate generated from pre-discovery evidence was: A conserved transformable quantity that appears in multiple forms and permits cross-domain accounting.
