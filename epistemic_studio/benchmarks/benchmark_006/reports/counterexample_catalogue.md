# Counterexample Catalogue

## Perfect-memory individual learner
- Violates: institutionalized_criticism
- Preserves function: persistent_memory, preserved_failures, applicability_boundaries
- Verdict: Can accumulate knowledge without social criticism if internal error update is unbiased.

## Collective hive mind
- Violates: institutionalized_criticism, question_refinement as discourse
- Preserves function: persistent_memory, knowledge_compression, applicability_boundaries
- Verdict: Social mechanisms collapse into internal cognitive functions; criticism is replaceable by internal model comparison.

## Non-symbolic gradient learner
- Violates: journal-like memory, explicit preserved failures, verbal question refinement
- Preserves function: temporal retention, negative error signal, compression, scope control
- Verdict: Violates human-readable forms but preserves primitive functions through weights and gradients.

## Evolution-only civilization
- Violates: persistent explicit memory, institutionalized criticism, question refinement
- Preserves function: retention in genomes, selection against failures, implicit applicability through niches
- Verdict: Cumulative adaptation occurs slowly without explicit science; it satisfies weaker knowledge accumulation but not explanatory science.

## Perfect quantum communication collective
- Violates: append-only records, journal, role separation
- Preserves function: shared state retention, instant comparison, scope tags
- Verdict: Communication changes implementations, not primitive requirements.

## Brute-force oracle search
- Violates: knowledge_compression, question_refinement
- Preserves function: retention, scope control
- Verdict: If exhaustive search and truth access are free, compression and refinement become convenient but unnecessary.

Strongest counterexample: non-symbolic gradient learners can accumulate predictive knowledge without journals, explicit hypotheses, or verbal criticism. They do not refute retention, error correction, or scope control; they refute the necessity of Studio-shaped implementations.
